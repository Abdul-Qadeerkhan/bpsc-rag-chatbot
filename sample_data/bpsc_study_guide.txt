BPSC EXAM PREPARATION GUIDE — BALOCHISTAN PUBLIC SERVICE COMMISSION
=======================================================================

ABOUT BPSC
----------
The Balochistan Public Service Commission (BPSC) conducts competitive examinations for recruitment to various civil service posts in the province of Balochistan, Pakistan. It was established under Article 242 of the Constitution of Pakistan.

EXAM STRUCTURE (BPS-14 to BPS-16)
-----------------------------------
Paper 1: English (100 marks)
- Essay Writing, Comprehension, Grammar, Précis

Paper 2: Pakistan Studies (100 marks)
- History of Pakistan, Geography, Constitution, Current Affairs

Paper 3: General Knowledge (100 marks)
- Everyday Science, Islamic Studies, Basic Mathematics, GK

Paper 4: Subject Specialist Paper (100 marks)
- Depends on the post applied for

BALOCHISTAN — KEY FACTS
------------------------
Capital: Quetta
Area: 347,190 sq km (largest province by area — 44% of Pakistan)
Population: Approximately 14.89 million (2023 census)
Official Languages: Urdu, English; Regional: Balochi, Brahui, Pashto

Districts of Balochistan (36 total):
Quetta, Pishin, Chaman, Zhob, Loralai, Khuzdar, Turbat, Gwadar, 
Kalat, Mastung, Nushki, Washuk, Panjgur, Dera Bugti, Sibi, 
Nasirabad, Jaffarabad, Bolan, Awaran, Kech, Lasbela, Hub, 
Kharan, Chaghi, Dalbandin, Ziarat, Harnai, Sherani, Musakhel,
Barkhan, Kohlu, Dera Murad Jamali, Sohbatpur, Lehri, Jhal Magsi

--------------------------

- Sulaiman Range (eastern border)

- Kirthar Range (east)

Major Rivers:
- Zhob River
- Bolan River (connects Quetta to Sibi/Indus plain)
- Hingol River (longest river, flows to Arabian Sea)
- Hab River (southern Balochistan)

Major Dams:
- Mirani Dam (Kech River, Turbat)
- Subakzai Dam (Quetta)
- Hab Dam (Hub)

PAKISTAN STUDIES — KEY TOPICS
-------------------------------
Creation of Pakistan:
- Pakistan achieved independence on 14 August 1947
- Muhammad Ali Jinnah became the first Governor-General
- Liaquat Ali Khan became the first Prime Minister
- The word "Pakistan" was coined by Chaudhry Rahmat Ali in 1933

Constitutions of Pakistan:
- Constitution of 1956: First constitution; Pakistan declared Islamic Republic
- Constitution of 1962: Presidential system under Ayub Khan
- Constitution of 1973: Current constitution; Parliamentary system; signed by Z.A. Bhutto

Important Passes in Pakistan:
- Khyber Pass: connects Pakistan to Afghanistan (KPK)
- Bolan Pass: connects Quetta to the Indus plain (Balochistan) — MOST IMPORTANT FOR BALOCHISTAN EXAM
- Khojak Pass: connects Quetta to Chaman/Afghanistan
- Tochi Pass: North Waziristan
- Gomal Pass: South Waziristan

ISLAMIC STUDIES — KEY FACTS
-----------------------------

1. Shahadah (Declaration of Faith)
2. Salat (Prayer — 5 times daily)
3. Zakat (Charity — 2.5% of savings)
4. Sawm (Fasting in Ramadan)
5. Hajj (Pilgrimage to Mecca — once in lifetime if able)

ENGLISH GRAMMAR TIPS
----------------------
Common Tenses:
- Simple Present: He walks to school.
- Present Continuous: He is walking to school.
- Simple Past: He walked to school.
- Past Perfect: He had walked to school.
- Future: He will walk to school.

Active vs Passive Voice:
- Active: "The teacher taught the lesson."
- Passive: "The lesson was taught by the teacher."

Key Prepositions:
- Interested IN (not "interested with")
- Afraid OF
- Responsible FOR
- Similar TO
- Different FROM

MATHEMATICS — BASIC FORMULAS
------------------------------
Area Formulas:
- Rectangle: Length × Width
- Triangle: ½ × Base × Height
- Circle: π × r²
- Square: Side²

Percentage:
- Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100
- If 25 out of 200 pass: (25/200) × 100 = 12.5%

Profit & Loss:
- Profit % = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100
- Loss % = (Loss / Cost Price) × 100

Speed, Distance, Time:
- Distance = Speed × Time
- Speed = Distance / Time
- Time = Distance / Speed

QUETTA — CITY FACTS
---------------------
- Quetta is the provincial capital of Balochistan
- Known as "Fruit Garden of Pakistan" due to fruit orchards
- Nickname: "Little Paris" (historically)
- Elevation: approximately 1,680 meters above sea level
- BUITEMS (Balochistan University of IT, Engineering and Management Sciences) is a major university
- University of Balochistan (UoB) is the oldest university in the province
- Quetta is connected to the rest of Pakistan via the Bolan Pass railway and road

CURRENT AFFAIRS PAKISTAN (2025-2026)
--------------------------------------
- Pakistan's 26th Constitutional Amendment was passed in 2024
- CPEC (China-Pakistan Economic Corridor) continues with Gwadar port development
- Pakistan's population crossed 250 million (2023 census: 249.5 million)
- Balochistan's main exports: natural gas, coal, chromite, gold, copper
- Reko Diq gold and copper mine project (Chaghi district) — one of world's largest

EXAM TIPS
----------
1. Focus on Balochistan-specific questions — BPSC always includes regional content
2. Pakistan Studies carries high weightage — memorize dates and constitutional facts
3. Islamic Studies questions are usually straightforward — know the Pillars and Quran facts
4. English section: practice précis writing (summarizing passages in 1/3rd the words)
5. For mathematics: practice percentage, ratio, and speed-distance-time problems
6. Current affairs: read Dawn newspaper online for recent Pakistan events
7. Past papers: Practice 5-10 years of past BPSC papers for pattern recognition
